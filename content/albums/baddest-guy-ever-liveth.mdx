Two albums in two years. The one where the confidence stopped sounding like
ambition and started sounding like fact. *Baddest Guy Ever Liveth* — BGEL
to anyone who lived through the rollout — arrived a year after YBNL and
closed the founding chapter of the label.

Pheelz handled nearly every beat; the guest list stayed closer to the
trenches than to radio's A-list. "Durosoke" and "Yemi My Lover" carried
the singles campaign, but the album's real argument was stamina: twenty-one
tracks of street-hop as mainstream language, delivered before most peers
had finished their second project. By the time the video awards caught up
in 2014, the upstart had become the standard.

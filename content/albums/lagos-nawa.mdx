A love letter to the city that raised the sound, closing out the
album-a-year streak. Seventeen tracks, made in forty-eight hours by
Olamide's own account, with Young Jonn on nearly every beat — a sprint
that somehow produced the decade's defining dance record.

"Wo" had already detonated that August: Bariga streets, the #WoChallenge,
shaku shaku going national. The album gathered the moment into a full
Lagos document — "Radio Lagos," the title track, Timaya and Reminisce on
"Bend It Over," Tiwa Savage on "Fine Fine Girls." The Street King run
didn't fade out. It danced offstage.

The EP as a lab: short, experimental, and a signal that the rules were
being rewritten. After two years of loose singles instead of full albums,
*999* arrived with almost no notice — nine tracks of hip-hop and trap
textures that refused the Street King formula.

Eight days later came the bigger news: YBNL's joint-venture distribution
deal with Empire. In hindsight the EP reads like a palate cleanser before
international infrastructure — a surprise drop that cleared space for
*Carpe Diem* to land on a wider stage.

Named after one of the largest known stars — and, he said, after his son.
A fitting title for the elder-statesman chapter. In March 2021 he hinted
he was done making albums; weeks later he announced *UY Scuti* anyway.
Ten tracks, released that June: pop, reggae and Afro-pop over the dense
street-hop blueprints of the previous decade.

"Rock" led the singles. Guests stayed lean — Fave, Jaywillz, Layydoe,
Phyno — and the album peaked on Billboard's World Albums chart. Pitchfork
and Pulse both filed reviews; the bigger story was the pattern: retirement
talk, then another record. The empire's founder was still writing.

Before the label, before the empire, there was a kid from Bariga with a
notebook full of Yoruba bars and a conviction the industry didn't share.
Under ID Cabasa's wing at Coded Tunes, Olamide cut his teeth on a Lagos
scene that still treated indigenous-language rap as a novelty.

*Rapsodi* (2011) is the document of that bet: a debut album rapped
largely in Yoruba, carried by the breakout single "Eni Duro". It didn't
just introduce a rapper — it argued that the street's own language could
lead Nigerian pop, not follow it.

This chapter covers the Coded Tunes years, the making of *Rapsodi*, and
the moment the bet started paying off.

Year three of the run — and the one he framed as a return to hardcore hip
hop. Announced as "no experimenting, just rap," *The Glory* arrived on
Boxing Day 2016 alongside OLIC at Eko Hotels, a victory-lap release timed
to a sold-out stage.

Features from Burna Boy, Wande Coal and Phyno mapped the moment: street
kings sharing the mic with the artists who would soon redefine the
mainstream. The album debuted at number six on Billboard's World Albums
chart — rare air for a YBNL street project — and later took Album of the
Year at the Nigeria Entertainment Awards. Glory, measured in both rooms
and charts.
